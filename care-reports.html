Report interpretation

Lab reports and prescriptions can be confusing and, sometimes, frightening. A clear explanation of what each value means — and what to do next — turns a page of numbers into a simple, practical plan.

Book an Appointment Call the Clinic

Making sense of your reports

Blood counts, sugar, cholesterol, thyroid, liver and kidney tests each tell part of the story. Reference ranges printed on a report are general guides — what a value means for you depends on your age, symptoms and history.

A single 'high' or 'low' figure rarely tells the whole picture. Reading the report alongside your symptoms and previous results is what makes the advice accurate and reassuring.

What we review

  • Blood test and urine reports
  • Current and past prescriptions
  • Specialist and hospital notes
  • Imaging and scan summaries
  • Warning signs to watch for
  • Clear, written next steps

How to get the most from a review

Bring all your reports and previous prescriptions, even old ones, so trends over time are clear. Note down your current medicines and any symptoms before the visit, and don't hesitate to ask questions until things make sense.

Keep a simple personal health file — paper or digital — so each report adds to the picture rather than starting from scratch every time.

What to do

  • Bring all reports and previous prescriptions
  • Note your symptoms and current medicines beforehand
  • Ask questions until the result is clear to you
  • Keep a personal health file of your records
  • Follow up on any recommended repeat tests

What to avoid

  • Don't panic over a single out-of-range value
  • Don't self-medicate based on internet searches
  • Don't skip recommended retests or follow-up
  • Don't compare your report directly with someone else's
  • Don't start or stop a medicine without advice

When to see the doctor

Come in soon if a report shows a markedly abnormal value, if you have new symptoms, if different advice from different sources is confusing you, or before you start or stop any medicine based on a result.
