Patient Education

Everyday health tips

Short, doctor-approved guidance for the most common questions raised at the clinic.

Diabetes

Fasting sugar above 130?

Don't skip your morning dose. Walk for 20 minutes after breakfast and recheck after three days. Bring the log to your next visit.

Fever

When fever needs review

Persistent fever beyond 72 hours, breathlessness, or rash should be examined the same day. Avoid self-prescribing antibiotics.

Blood Pressure

Measuring BP correctly

Sit quietly for 5 minutes, feet on floor, arm at heart level. Take two readings a minute apart and record both for review.

Acidity

Recurring acidity?

Avoid late dinners, fried food, and habitual painkiller use. If symptoms persist beyond two weeks, schedule an evaluation.

FAQ

Common patient questions

Quick answers to help you plan a visit, prepare reports, and understand consultation flow.

Do I need an appointment, or can I walk in?

Walk-ins are accepted whenever slots are free, but booking ahead via the appointment form or phone call shortens the waiting time considerably.

What should I carry for my first visit?

A photo ID, your current medicines, and any recent lab reports or prescriptions. For diabetic or hypertensive patients, a home log of readings helps.

Is video consultation available?

Yes. Video consultations are offered at ₹300, ideal for follow-ups, prescription review, and report interpretation. Call the clinic to schedule.

Do you treat children?

The clinic treats older children and adolescents for general illnesses. For infants and young children, a paediatrician is recommended for ongoing care.

What if I have an emergency?

For chest pain, severe breathlessness, stroke symptoms, major injury, or unconsciousness, please go directly to the nearest emergency department.

How long is a typical consultation?

First visits usually take 15–20 minutes. Follow-ups are shorter. Time is given as needed — chronic disease reviews are not rushed.
