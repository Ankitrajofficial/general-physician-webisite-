Health Reminders

Small steps that support better care

These reminders help patients prepare for a safer, more productive consultation.

Diabetes

Bring your sugar log

Recent fasting and post-meal readings help the doctor understand patterns and adjust follow-up advice.

Fever

Track warning signs

Note fever duration, breathing symptoms, rash, dehydration, travel, and medicines already taken.

Blood Pressure

Record home readings

Bring two or three days of readings, along with timing and current medicines, for a clearer review.

Medication

Carry current medicines

Photos or strips of active medicines reduce confusion and help prevent duplicate prescriptions.

FAQ

Common patient questions

Quick answers to help you plan a visit, prepare reports, and understand consultation flow.

Do I need an appointment, or can I walk in?

Walk-ins are accepted whenever slots are free, but booking ahead by phone or the appointment form helps reduce waiting time.

What should I carry for my first visit?

Carry a photo ID, current medicines, recent lab reports, previous prescriptions, and home readings for diabetes or blood pressure if available.

Is video consultation available?

Yes. Video consultation is available for INR 300 and is best suited for follow-ups, prescription review, and report interpretation.

Can I get documents for work or reimbursement?

Receipts, notes, referrals, and certificates can be provided after clinical review when appropriate. Call ahead for specific paperwork needs.

What if I have an emergency?

This website and appointment form are not for emergencies. For severe symptoms, go to the nearest emergency department immediately.

How long is a typical consultation?

First visits usually take longer than follow-ups. Chronic disease reviews may need extra time for medicine and report review.
